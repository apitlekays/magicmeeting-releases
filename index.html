For qualitative researchers

Code your interviews, the way the manual teaches.

MagicMeeting's notes are built around the qualitative coding workflow — assign a code to any excerpt, group codes into categories and themes, and keep analytic memos as your interpretation develops.

Structured around the codes-to-categories model in Johnny Saldaña, The Coding Manual for Qualitative Researchers.
  • Highlight a passage and code it in place — no exporting to another tool.
  • Nest codes into parent categories to move from first-cycle codes toward themes.
  • Every coded excerpt keeps its speaker and timestamp — one click plays the audio.
  • Interview data never leaves your machine — helpful for consent, ethics, and IRB commitments.
  • Export your codebook and coded excerpts to PDF or Word for write-up.

Questions

Frequently asked

Is my audio really never uploaded?

Correct. There's no server and no account. After a one-time model download, MagicMeeting runs fully offline — pull the network cable and it works identically.

Can I use this for qualitative research?

Yes — that's a core use case. Transcribe interviews and focus groups, then code excerpts into categories and themes with analytic memos, each linked back to the speaker and timestamp. The workflow follows the codes-to-categories model in Saldaña's Coding Manual, and you can export your coded notes to PDF or Word.

Where are my recordings stored?

In a local database on your Mac, under your control. Delete a session and its audio, transcript, speakers, and notes are gone for good — helpful for consent and IRB commitments.

Do I need a subscription or license key?

No. Download it and go — it's free.

Can it transcribe files I already recorded?

Yes — point it at an audio file and get a full speaker-labeled transcript you can edit, code, and export.

Which platforms are supported?

Any Mac on macOS 12.3+ today (Apple Silicon or Intel, one universal download). Native Windows and Linux builds are targeted for Q3 2026 — watch the repository to be notified.
